Drawing on feminist and critical theoretical frameworks, it argues that the cultural impulse to condem image manipulation overlooks its role in shaping political subject formation within the constraints of capitalist, democratic society. Specifically, this thesis analyzes how the core values of authenticity, consumerism, spectacle, and apology surface in varied practices of gendered image manipulation, engaging and shaping producers and consumers alike as political subjects." fpath: "ugthesis-cover.png" image_alt: "Screenshot of the online publication of the thesis, including the thesis's title, author, and date of publication." content_type: "Writing" skills: "Feminist theory, Critical theory, Visual analysis" portfolio: "y" view: "https://scholarworks.umb.edu/cgi/viewcontent.cgi?article=1023&context=honors_theses" --- {% include navbar.html %}
